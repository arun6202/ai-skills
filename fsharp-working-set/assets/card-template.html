Where the kit runs thin

The batteries Python still owns.

No boosterism. These are the corners where Python's depth, community, and sheer surface area still win, and where reaching for .NET interop is a compromise rather than a pleasure.

Exploratory data science

pandas/seaborn muscle memory and notebook-density. Deedle + Plotly.NET are good; the ecosystem around them is not Python's.

The scientific stack

scipy / statsmodels breadth. Math.NET and FSharp.Stats cover the common path, not the long tail of specialised methods.

Pretrained-model ecosystems

grab-a-model-and-go. TorchSharp and ML.NET work, but the model zoo and tutorials live in Python.

Glue-script velocity

throwaway one-file scripts. F# scripting (.fsx) is real but the dynamic-glue corner is Python's home turf.

F# wins the opposite trade: when the pipeline itself must be typed, total, and provable — your domain exactly. Spend the freedom there.
